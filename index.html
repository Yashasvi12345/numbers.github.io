Real Numbers

Divisibility: A non zero integer 'a' is said to divide an integer 'b' if there exists an integer 'c' such that b=ac. The integer 'b' is called dividend, integer 'a' is said to be the divisor and the integer 'c' is said to be the quotient

For Example: 5 divides 35 because there is an integer 7 such that 35=5*7

Euclid's Division Algorithm: For any integer 'a'. a=bq+r where b=divisor and r=remainder. The remainder can be 0,1,2....b-1

For Example: Let an integer 'a' be divided by 2
                                a=2q+r    where r=0,1(2-1)
Case 1: R=0            a=2q+0 = 2q
Case 2: R=1            a=2q+1

.·. We can conclude that all integers are in the form of 2q or 2q+1 and every even integer is of the form 2q and every odd integer is of the form 2q+1
